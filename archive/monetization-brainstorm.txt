Edit
Looking at your trivia discovery app, here are several monetization strategies that could work well:

Immediate Revenue Streams:

Venue Partnerships - This is your biggest opportunity. Charge bars/restaurants $50-200/month for premium listings with photos, detailed descriptions, and contact info. Offer "featured venue" spots for $100-300/month that appear at the top of search results.

Event Promotion - Let venues pay to promote special trivia events, themed nights, or tournaments. Think $25-75 per promoted event.

Commission Model - Partner with venues to take a small cut (5-10%) of cover charges or food/drink minimums when people book through your app.

Freemium User Features - Charge users $3-5/month for features like: saving favorite venues, RSVP tracking, team formation tools, leaderboards across venues, or early access to special events.

Local Advertising - Sell banner ads to local businesses near trivia venues - restaurants, rideshare promos, entertainment venues. Start at $200-500/month per market.

Scaling Strategies:

White Label Solution - License your platform to trivia companies or venue chains for $500-2000/month per market.

Data Analytics - Sell anonymized foot traffic and demographic data to venues and local businesses.

Merchandise/Affiliate - Partner with trivia supply companies, local food delivery, or entertainment booking.

Your competitive advantage is being hyper-focused on trivia while Untappd does beer and Bandsintown does concerts. That specialization lets you charge premium rates to a passionate, engaged audience.

Start with venue partnerships - that's where the real money is.
